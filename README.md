## What Is an Old Fashioned?

The Old Fashioned is one of the oldest cocktails in the world.

Traditionally, it combines bourbon or rye whiskey, sugar, aromatic bitters, and a twist of orange peel. The ingredients are minimal, but the flavor is surprisingly complex.

Warm vanilla notes from the bourbon. A touch of sweetness. Citrus oils. A little spice from the bitters.

Nothing extra. Nothing wasted.

## Why This Easy Old Fashioned Recipe Works

Many cocktails hide the base spirit.

The Old Fashioned does the opposite.

It highlights the whiskey and gently rounds its edges. The sugar softens the intensity. Bitters add depth. Orange brightens everything at the end.

It feels refined, yet it is incredibly easy to make.

And honestly, once you master it, you’ll wonder why you ever paid bar prices.

## Ingredients
2 ounces bourbon
1 sugar cube or 1 teaspoon simple syrup
2 to 3 dashes aromatic bitters
1 teaspoon water (if using a sugar cube)
Ice
Orange peel
Optional: one cherry

That’s all you need.

## Best Bourbon for an Old Fashioned

Choose a bourbon you enjoy sipping.

It doesn’t need to be expensive. Just smooth and flavorful.

Good bourbons often bring notes of caramel, vanilla, oak, and a little spice. These flavors shine beautifully in an Old Fashioned.

If you prefer a bolder drink, rye whiskey works wonderfully too.

## How to Make an Easy Old Fashioned
## Step 1: Add Sugar and Bitters

Place the sugar cube in a rocks glass.

Add the bitters and water.

If using simple syrup, simply combine it with the bitters.

## Step 2: Dissolve the Sugar

Muddle gently until the sugar mostly dissolves.

No need to be aggressive. The drink rewards patience.

## Step 3: Add Ice

Use one large cube if possible.

Large cubes melt more slowly, keeping the drink colder without watering it down too fast.

## Step 4: Pour in the Bourbon

Add 2 ounces of bourbon.

The aroma begins immediately. Warm and inviting.

## Step 5: Stir

Stir for 20 to 30 seconds.

This chills the cocktail and adds just enough dilution to balance the flavors.

## Step 6: Garnish

Twist an orange peel over the glass to release the oils.

Rub it around the rim and drop it in.

Optional cherry. Nice, but not required.

## What Does an Old Fashioned Taste Like?

Strong, but smooth.

Sweet, but not sugary.

The bourbon leads. Sugar rounds the edges. Bitters add complexity. Orange peel lifts everything with a bright citrus note.

It tastes like a cocktail with nothing to prove.

## Tips for the Perfect Old Fashioned
## Use Quality Ice

Ice matters more than most people realize.

Large, solid cubes melt slowly and preserve the drink’s balance.

## Don’t Over-Sweeten

The Old Fashioned should showcase the bourbon, not bury it.

A little sugar goes a long way.

## Express the Orange Peel

This tiny step adds remarkable aroma and freshness.

Do not skip it if you can help it.

## Stir, Don’t Shake

Shaking introduces air and too much dilution.

Stirring keeps the texture silky and polished.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
## Too Much Sugar

This turns a sophisticated cocktail into something sticky and heavy.

## Low-Quality Whiskey

Because the recipe is so simple, every ingredient stands out.

## Too Many Garnishes

One orange peel is usually enough.

Sometimes less really is more.

## Easy Variations to Try

Once you master the classic, a few small changes can create exciting new flavors.

## Maple Old Fashioned

Replace sugar with a teaspoon of maple syrup.

Warm. Cozy. Slightly richer.

## Smoked Old Fashioned

Add a hint of smoke for a dramatic presentation and deeper aroma.

## Rum Old Fashioned

Swap bourbon for aged rum for a sweeter, softer interpretation.

Unexpected. Very enjoyable.

## Bourbon or Rye—Which Is Better?
